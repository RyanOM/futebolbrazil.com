Brasileirao - Round 27

Paul McDaniel October 8, 2018 Brasileirão

A brilliant injury-time double save by Botafogo goalkeeper Saulo denied Sao Paulo all three points and the opportunity to regain top spot as O Tricolor had to twice come from behind to salvage a point in an engrossing 2-2 draw in Rio’s Olympic Stadium on Sunday.

Earlier in the day, a commanding performance from Palmeiras swept aside Cruzeiro with goals from Lima, Hyoran and Gomez contributing to a 3-1 win, a result which saw Luiz Felipe Scolari’s men climb to the top of the table and ultimately end the weekend in first place following Sao Paulo’s third successive draw.

More bad news for fans of Diego Aguirre’s team was to follow at full-time as Andres D’Alessandro’s 86th minute penalty earned maximum points for fellow title hopefuls Internacional after they came from behind to beat Vitoria 2-1 in Porto Alegre.

Elsewhere, supporters who attended Atletico-MG and Sport Recife’s encounter were treated to seven goals as Atletico ran out 5-2 winners courtesy of five different scorers. Corinthians underwhelming season continued with a 0-0 draw away at America-MG on Saturday evening, while Flamengo, whose title aspirations have faded in the past few weeks, and Bahia also shut each other out.

Gremio claimed the points away at Fluminese, Everton scoring deep into injury time to secure a 0-1 win. Santos left it even later to beat Atletico-PR with Carlos Sanchez scoring the decisive penalty in the 97th minute of a 1-0 victory. At the bottom end of the table, Ceara’s improved form continued with an ultimately comfortable 3-1 win over Chapecoense, which lifted them out of the relegation zone and into 15th place.

Parana were unable to capitalise on a fast start against Vasco da Gama in the last match of the round on Monday night, and had to settle for a point after Maximilliano Lopez’s penalty just before half-time cancelled out Alex Santana’s 9th minute opener.
