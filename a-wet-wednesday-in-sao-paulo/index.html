A Wet Wednesday in Sao Paulo

Chris Blair October 4, 2018 Bahia Copa Brazil Palmeiras

“But could he hack it on a wet Wednesday in Stoke?”. The oft asked, sometimes badly answered question of Messi et al, when lazy journalists and ex-pros debate the merits of South Americans being able to handle the rigours of facing Ryan Shawcross and his band of merry hatchet-men in the apparently torrential middle of England. The answer, in case anyone is wondering, is yes. I can answer this with authority because:

A. Messi is quite good at football and Stoke aren’t, and
B. Football in Brazil isn’t all sunshine and rainbows on the beach.

This brings me to my first experience of the Copa Brasil. This was indeed a wet Wednesday, not in the Potteries but in Sao Paulo. Played at Pacaembu, Sao Paulo’s city owned stadium-for-hire-when-some-popstar-has-booked-yours, we set off for a night of football viewed from the terracing and in 10 degree drizzle. Terracing, still somewhat a novelty for a British fan whose football watching was for the most part post-Hillsborough, holds a certain magic quality. It harks back to days when football was played by men who enjoyed pints after a game (sometimes before and during). Days that probably never really existed, but that in imagination hold the key to “real football”, before the gentrification with such onerous afflictions as 'safety' and 'women’s toilets'. An uncovered antiquity holding 40k, the Pacembu hosted the Word Cup - first time round - and has, needless to say, seen more glamorous fixtures than this quarter-final between Palmeiras and Bahia. A must win for a newly returned Scolari, the game started with optimism from the Palmeiras fans.

As the rain kicked in and Os Porcos were unable to assert any form of dominance, this hope soon turned to frustration and several "Nossa Senhoras!" from the less than impressed crowd. With time ticking on, and the very near and terrifying prospect of extra time, penalties and a cold approaching, finally the deadlock was broken by Dudu. A chilly Sao Paulo winter’s evening was finally warmed by the only decent piece of play, and the idea of being in my toasty bed was now becoming real. The final twenty minutes was played out on a slick yet slow surface, and with my prayers for no further goals silently murmured, the seconds dripped away like the rain through my inappropriately optimistic Celtic hoodie. These prayers were answered, and as we traipsed out of the Pacembu I was left contemplating if Peter Crouch could have hacked this turgid affair on a wet Wednesday in Sao Paulo. Would Stephen Ireland* have invented another granny and her untimely demise to get out of the rain? Would Darren Fletcher be dreaming of the tropical climes of Edinburgh? Would Saido Berahino** be able to keep up his goal per 300 game ratio? I guess we will never know.

*At time of writing Stephen Ireland is currently no longer at Stoke, having been released after relegation. The status of his grandmothers, imaginary or not, is unknown.

** After writing, but before we got our arses in gear to get a website started up, Saido Beranhino (sounds quite Brazilian, no?) scored a goal. He is still shite.
