Brasileirao - Round 28

Paul McDaniel October 8, 2018 Brasileirão

Palmeiras tightened their grip on the top spot as they scored two goals in quick succession to beat Sao Paulo 0-2 at the Morumbi on Saturday evening. Despite being only their fourth defeat of the campaign, and their first at home in the league since August 2017, Sao Paulo are now four points behind the leaders and paying the price for recent disappointing draws against middling opponents.

O Verdao went into the derby knowing that victory would put daylight between themselves and the chasing pack following Internacional’s surprise 2-1 loss away at Sport Recife on Friday night. A late goal from Mateus completed an improbable comeback for the league’s second-bottom side after Nicolas Lopez had given the away team the lead.

Also on Friday night, Flamengo put themselves firmly back into the race for the title as they crushed Corinthians 0-3 at the Itaquerao, inflicting the heaviest home defeat O Timao have suffered since moving into the stadium in 2014.

Rounding off Friday’s action, Santos beat Vitoria 0-1 courtesy of Carlos Sanchez’s ealy goal, which made it two in two for the Uruguayan midfielder. Vitoria’s Maurice was shown a straight red card late on for using his arm in a challenge with Diego Pituca.

Atletico-PR scored within a minute of their match against America-MG, and it proved to be a long afternoon for the team from Minas Gerais as Atletico-PR went on to score three more times in a comfortable 4-0 home victory. After last week’s rip-roaring 5-2 victory over Sport Recife, Atletico-MG came crashing back down to Earth with a disappointing 1-0 loss away at strugglers Chapecoense.

Honours were shared in Porto Alegre on Sunday night, as Gremio and Bahia played out an entertaining 2-2 draw, which saw Bahia’s Jackson receive his marching orders following a second yellow card ten minutes after the break. Elber had given Bahia a third minute lead, the striker’s first goal since May, and he then extended the advantage with a second goal in the 67th minute, but ten-man Bahia were unable to hold on and Gremio secured a point with Jael’s 88th minute penalty after Juninho Capixaba had given them hope with a 85th minute strike.

Monday night’s game saw Fluminese hosting, and soundly beating, the league's bottom club Parana. A brace from Jadson sandwiched a goal from Richard Coelho, with a fourth added five minutes before the end by Luciano.

The Rio derby between Botafogo and Vasco da Gama took place on Tuesday evening, owing to Botafogo's midweek involvement in the Copa do Brasil, and ended 1-1, with Maximiliano Lopez cancelling out Luis Fernando Moraes dos Santos' opener for Botafogo.
