Brasileirao - Round 31

Paul McDaniel October 28, 2018 Brasileirão

This week’s action took place over Friday, Saturday and Monday due to the Brazilian presidential election, which was held on Sunday.

All eyes were on the world-renowned Maracana as the top two sides in the country, Flamengo and Palmeiras, squared up for what promised to be a potential title-decider. Flamengo needed to avoid defeat at all costs in order to keep Palmeiras within touching distance, and they were able to do that courtesy of Colombian Marlos Moreno’s equaliser after Dudu had put O Verdao into a second-half lead. Flamengo remain four points behind the leaders with seven games left to play.

Friday’s matches saw an out-of-sorts Sao Paulo visit Vitoria, while in Rio de Janeiro Vasco da Gama hosted Internacional. Sao Paulo desperately needed to get their faltering season back on track with a win against the strugglers from Salvador, and they had Bruno Alves to thank for their first victory in seven games - the centre-back sweeping home a cut-back in the 36th minute. It wasn’t all good news for the team in white however, as goalkeeper Jean, who previously represented Vitoria’s city rivals Bahia, was dismissed after the final whistle for provoking the home fans, sparking angry scenes on the pitch. Internacional blew the chance to move into second place as they could only manage a draw on Friday evening. Uruguayan striker Jonatan Alvez had given them the lead in the 85th minute, only for Maximiliano Lopez to score his fourth in five games with an injury-time penalty. Internacional also had a player sent-off very late on, with Edenilson receiving his second yellow card in the 95th minute of a match beset by contentious decisions from the referee.

On Saturday afternoon, Santos put Fluminese to the sword with three goals in the last six minutes of their home tie at the Estadio Urbano Caldeira. Chapecoense kept themselves in the struggle to avoid the drop by beating America-MG at home - Wellington Paulista grabbing the vital goal in the 60th minute. Corinthians were able to arrest any further decline in their standing with Danilo playing a starring role from the bench and grabbing both goals in a 2-1 victory. Cruzeiro eased to a 3-1 victory over Parana, who could be relegated at the end of Round 33, if results do not go their way. Atletico-PR’s good run of form continued with a 2-1 comeback win over Botafogo, a result which sees the Rio-based team just one point above the relegation zone.

The match of the day, as far as the neutral is concerned, came at the Arena do Gremio where Sport Recife were looking to build on last week’s win against Vasco da Gama, as they seek an improbable escape from relegation. Seven goals from six different goal-scorers saw Sport throw away a two-goal lead, before regaining the advantage with Gabriel’s 53rd minute strike, only to lose it again just minutes later as Thonny Anderson equalised for Gremio. Not to be deterred, Sport kept looking for a way through, and in the 64th minute their endeavour was rewarded when Jair claimed all three points for the away side.
