Brasileirao - Round 32

Paul McDaniel November 5, 2018 Brasileirão

Palmeiras took a huge stride towards securing the title on Saturday evening as they came out on top in an exciting 3-2 win over Santos. This looked to be a tricky tie for the leaders, with Santos on a good run of form and the team in green still nursing a potential hangover after their midweek semi-final exit from the Copa Libertadores at the hands of Boca Juniors. If they were feeling tired after their exertions against the Argentines, they showed no sign in the first half as they went into the interval two goals to the good, courtesy of Dudu and Edu Dracena. With the game very much in their grasp, things took a turn when Jonathan Copete clawed one back for Santos on 55 minutes. Ten minutes later and it was game on as Dodo levelled for O Peixe. The lead was not to last, however, as Vitor Luis grabbed the winning goal from a free-kick in the 71st minute. There was still time for more drama as Santos’ Diego Pituca picked up a second yellow card and was dismissed ten minutes from time.

Saturday’s other games were not quite so dramatic. Gremio and Vasco da Gama both winning 0-1 away at Atletico-MG and Fluminese respectively. Pedro Geromel struck early for Gremio, while Maxi Lopez’s fine run of form continued with his fifth goal in his last six games. Atletico-MG had Ecuador midfield Junior Sornoza dismissed deep into injury time to compound the loss.

Sunday afternoon saw fourth host second as Sao Paulo welcomed Flamengo to the Morumbi. Like Saturday’s main attraction, this clash between two of this season’s title contenders didn’t disappoint with four goals and leads won and lost. Diego Souza opened the scoring for Sao Paulo with his eleventh goal of the season, only for the hosts to be pegged back two minutes later as Fernando Uribe netted an equaliser in the tenth minute. After the break, Helio Junio must have though he’d done enough to secure all three points for Sao Paulo, only for Rodinei to score in the 80th minute and draw the team from Rio level once again.

The other big game on Saturday took place in Porto Alegre as Internacional faced Atletico-PR. A win would move the home team up to second place, but for most of the match it looked like it would be another missed opportunity for Odair Hellmann’s team as Camancho scored for Atletico-PR in the 62nd minute. Rodrigo Moledo equalised in the 82nd minute, and it looked like Internacional would have to settle for a point, but they were awarded a 94th minute penalty, which was duly converted by Andres D’Alessandro. His team are now just five points behind Palmeiras with six games left to play.

Elsewhere, Parana and Vitoria played out a 1-1 draw, which did neither side any favours in their battle against the drop, but did halt a run of four successive losses for the league’s bottom side. Parana are now winless in 18 games, and Serie B bound barring a miracle involving a run of six successive wins and a serious goal difference overhaul. America-MG also remain in imminent danger of the drop as they failed to pick up any points at home to Cruzeiro, losing 1-2 on the night.

Remarkably, last season’s champions Corinthians are still not out of danger and a couple more results like Sunday evening’s loss to Botafogo may well see O Timao sweating over results elsewhere. Rodrigo Lindoso scored the only goal of the game for the hosts, a result which pulled Botafogo further clear of the relegation zone. Bahia condemned Chapecoense to defeat by the same scoreline, Elber getting the vital goal and helping his team to the relative comfort of 11th place while leaving Chapecoense firmly in the drop zone.  

On Monday evening, Sport Recife continued their recent renaissance with a narrow win over fellow strugglers Ceara. Gabriel scored the all important goal in the 53rd minute, helping his side to their fourth win in the last five games and, most importantly, lifting them out of the bottom four.
