Brasileirao - Round 30

Paul McDaniel October 22, 2018 Brasileirão

Palmeiras managed to hold on to their lead and secure all three points in Sunday’s 2-1 victory over Ceara, despite having forward Deyverson dismissed in first-half injury time for an inexplicable chest-high studs-up challenge on Richardson. A double from Bruno Henrique was enough for the home team, Arthur replying for Ceara in the 55th minute.

Title rivals Flamengo kept the pressure on with an impressive result away at bottom side Parana. Three second-half strikes followed Lucas Paqueta’s 19th minute opener to give O Mengao a comfortable trip back to Rio de Janeiro, and keep the gap at the top down to four points.

Saturday’s matches saw America-MG play out yet another draw, their fifth in seven games, with Gremio. The match finished 1-1 with Jean Pyerre’s penalty drawing Gremio level after Juninho scored for the hosts. Bahia took maximum points from their trip to Botafogo, courtesy of Edigar Junio’s strike just minutes after coming on as a second-half sub. Sao Paulo and Atletico-PR were unable to find a way past each other in an unremarkable game at the Morumbi stadium, which must surely spell the end of any lingering title aspirations for O Tricolor. Sport Recife meanwhile earned a vital win in their attempt to avoid relegation, beating Vasco da Game 2-1 and with enough to spare to afford a 94th minute penalty miss by Gabriel.

On Sunday afternoon, Vitoria hosted an out-of-form Corinthians side and played their part in an entertaining 2-2 draw, with a goal apiece scored in a frantic injury-time period. Roger thought that he had secured the win for the Sao Paulo-based side with his 91st minute strike, until Neilton snatched a point for Vitoria in the 93rd minute. Fluminese beat Atletico-MG 1-0 with Luciano scoring in the 63rd minute after Fabio Santos had missed a penalty for Atletico-MG at the start of the second half. Cruzeiro had no problem dispatching Chapecoense on Sunday evening, with three first-half goals making for an easy three points.

The round’s last fixtures saw Internacional host Santos hoping to keep within touching distance of the top two, and Ceara and Crumeabzeiro catching up on a postponed fixture. Internacional will be disappointed not to have taken all three points after leading twice in their match, but an own goal from right-back Fabiano meant the game finished 2-2. Arthur added to the goal he scored in the loss to Palmeiras, helping himself to both goals in a crucial 0-2 victory, which lifted his side from 17th to 14th and a point clear of the relegation zone with eight games to go.
