Brasileirao - Round 29

Paul McDaniel October 18, 2018 Brasileirão

Round 29’s action kicked off in the Maracana with the Rio derby between Flamengo and Fluminese. Title hopefuls Flamengo brushed aside their city rivals in a comfortable 3-0 win, with Fernando Uribe bagging a double either side of a headed effort from defender Leonardo Duarte da Silva.

Another derby, 275 miles down the coast in Santos, saw the home team condemn Corinthians to yet another defeat, their 12th of the season, beating their big-city opponents 1-0 courtesy of Gabriel Barbosa Almeida’s 14th goal of the campaign. Corinthians have now lost their last four games in all competitions, scoring just once in their past five, heaping further pressure on manager Jair Ventura who only joined the side in September. Saturday’s final fixture saw Bahia beat Parana 2-0, with a late brace from Vinicius pulling the team from the north-east clear of the relegation zone.

Sunday afternoon was dominated by two fixtures involving teams in the top 6, with both matches kicking off at 4pm: Internacional hosted Sao Paulo, while Palmeiras welcomed Gremio to Allianz Parque. Sao Paulo raced into an early lead, Liziero finishing off a fine move with a clever finish, but that was as good as it got for the visitors as Internacional took control of the game and ended up comfortably winning the tie 3-1. Two goals from Leandro Damiao came before a 95th penalty from Nicolas Lopez, awarded after a clumsy challenge from Anderson Martins, which also resulted in a second yellow card for the centre-back.

Palmeiras stretched their unbeaten run to fourteen games with little drama, beating 5th placed Gremio 2-0 with Deyverson grabbing both goals. Palmeiras have conceded just three goals in the league since losing 1-0 away at Fluminese at the end of July, and it is increasingly difficult to see any way that they can be stopped from winning back the title they last held in 2016.

In Sunday’s other matches, Vitoria won 0-1 away at Chapecoense to halt a four-game losing streak and drag themselves out of the relegation places. Vasco da Gama took all three points against Cruzeiro, strikes from Yago Pikachu and Maximiliano Lopez proving the difference. Atletico-PR trounced Sport Recife 4-0 with all of the goals coming in the second half. The Minas Gerais derby did not match its Rio and Sao Paulo based counterparts for entertainment, as Atletico-MG and America-MG played out a scoreless 90 minutes. Botafogo’s Monday night visit to Ceara also ended 0-0 after Arthur missed a penalty for the hosts in the 31st minute.
