Back to Business

Chris Blair April 26, 2019

The new season is upon us  as the state championships make way for the Brasileirao. Futebol Brazil has awoken from its summer slumber ready to fill you in on the season that lies ahead.


Big Moves


The most notable in-league transfer was that of Gabigol from Santos to Flamengo via his parent club Inter Milan. He has hit the ground running with 2 goals in 3 games in the Copa Libertadores and will be hoping to top the scoring charts again while pushing a Lucas Paqueta less Flamengo one step further than last years runner up place.


Another return to Brazil for the once prodigal son Alexandre Pato marked the biggest coup from outside the league. Now in his third stint playing in his homeland the former Selecao star will be looking to continue his impressive scoring for from his days in China when he returns to Paulista runners up Sao Paulo.


Champions Palmeiras can count the signing of a contract extension of last years Golden Ball winner Dudu as their best piece of non-transfer transfer business. Their other big move was to bring in Gustavo Gomez from AC Milan on loan.


Ones to look out for


Last season saw three more big names make the annual migration to Europe. With Vinicius now establishing himself with Real Madrid his soon to be teammate will have until June of this season to say his goodbyes to Brazil. It will be interesting to see how much game time and what sort of treatment last seasons “most promising newcomer” will receive.



Title Tilt


Hstory dictates that we are likely to get our winners from the greater Sao Paulo or Rio area. Last season’s league went down to the penultimate game and this seasons should be just as exciting. As mentioned before second place Flamengo have added guaranteed goals to their title tilt and Palmerias have retained their prized assets.


As good a guide as any is to look at the Copa Libertadores which is already 4 games in. Last season title dark horses International are running away with their group which contains last years continental champions River Plate no less. Athletico Paranese are similarly topping a group with illustrious opposition in Boca Jrs. Perhaps the standout performances are coming fro Cruziero who have won five from five playing some exciting football. Last years Copa do Brazil champions are Futebol Brazil’s tip for a team to watch this season.


New Boys


Up from Seria B come CSA, Goias, Avai and Seria B champion Fortaleza. It will be a baptism of fire for Fortaleza as they meet Palmerias at the ALianz this Sunday. CSA will host the Porcos in the following game week so we should have an early indicator how these two will fare with the step up.
